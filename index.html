Climate Change

How is it affecting us?

Climate Change refers to the broad range of global phenomena created predominantly by fossil fuels that add excess heat into the atmosphere. This excess heat causes temperature rising and higher sea levels, both life-threatening weather events according to NASA. This increase in temperatures in the last mid-20th century is 95% attributed to human action. Since the late 19th century the Earth's average surface temperature has risen by 1.62 degrees Fahrenheit. In fact, the year 2016 is the warmest year on record. These rises in temperature have led to decreasing ice sheets in Antarctica and Greenland. On average, Antarctica and Greenland 286 billion tons of ice per year from 1993-2016. Gobal sea levels have risen 8 inches in the last century. These changes in cliamte will in the near future shorten growing seasons, lead to stronger hurricanes or other extreme weather events, increase heat waves, and it is projected to lead to an "Ice-Free Arctic". It is predicted by Climate Scientists that by 2040 if no real, drastic change is put in place then then the effects will be "baked" into the natural systems of the planet.

Gun Control

How is it affecting us?

Gun control is defined as the regulation of the sale and use of firearms by civilians. Many countries have strict regulations to prevent incidents which can result in multiple lives lost. Gun control however is a very controversial topic, especially in America. Students and parents demand for action. Then, people forget about the occurrence and unfortunately, a new shooting takes place and the debate occurs. However, after Stoneman Douglas High School shooting, commonly known as the Parkland Shooting, more demand for change happened. Protests erupted and students all over the country, walked out to protest. A country wide movement, known as the March Of Our Lives. The March of Our Lives was a student-led protest in order to prevent gun violence in the United States. It was planned not a while after the Parkland shooting left seventeen students and teachers dead in total. The protest was “the largest single-day protest in the history of the nation’s capital,” according to USA Today. The shooting inspired not only student survivors, but moved parents, and students from all over the country, to form a movement in hopes of persuading lawmakers to address the issue of gun violence in and outside of schools. Due to this movement, The US Department of Justice progressed to ban bump stocks, an attachment that makes a semiautomatic rifle to fire faster, in March of 2018. Bump stocks were not used in the Parkland shooting, but it was used in the fatal 2017 shooting in Las Vegas, the deadliest one in modern US history. This was a big step, but more can be done.

What can we do?

To help prevent future shootings, donating to gun control groups can be a huge help. Everytown for Gun Safety works to prevent gun violence through raising awareness and promoting gun-control codification. Another campaign is the Brady Campaign, which aims to cut gun deaths in half by 2025; the Coalition to Stop Gun Violence, which use policy development and advocacy to prevent shooting violence; and the Giffords Law Center, which was founded by former congresswoman Gabby Giffords — who was shot in 2011 — and her husband Mark Kelly. Additionally, calling out politicians for taking money from the gun lobby, you can also reach out to your elected officials to let them know how important gun-control is to you and other people.
